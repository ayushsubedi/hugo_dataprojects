APPLIED ECONOMETRICS FOR PRACTITIONERS

This course aims to train and equip young professionals with the tools and techniques of applied statistics and econometrics so they are able to carry out empirical analysis on diverse issues. The training, divided into two phases, focuses on the issues related to international development and domestic social issues-- poverty, education, health, agriculture, labor issues-- of Nepal, and relies on exercises and examples from the Nepal Living Standard Survey (NLSS 2011) data. It covers some advanced, but commonly needed, methods of statistical analysis including cross-sectional econometric analysis and discussion on panel data analysis. Overall, its emphasis is on the application, not derivation, of methodology.

By the end of the course, the participants will be able to answer research question such as: What factors are associated with the poverty outcomes in Nepal? They will also be able to critically review and correctly interpret regression results. Moreover participants will develop a habit of working on Stata dofile and be comfortable implementing the econometric software: Stata-12.

NEPAL DATA LITERACY PROGRAM

Nepal is undergoing a historic transition toward a federal nation, bringing decision making closer to people and providing an opportunity for the government to improve its service delivery. Amidst the transition, data literacy can support Nepal Government’s priorities by fostering an environment where citizens have the knowledge to use data for evidence-based decision making. It can lead to innovative solutions that promote economic growth and improve living conditions of people.

Bearing this in mind, Nepal Data Literacy Program is designed to catalyze stronger data driven decision-making by government and non-government actors through targeted data literacy workshops. The program comprises a 100-hour modular, customizable pedagogy develop technical skills and enhance a ‘culture of data use’ among Nepalis.

The program comprises of a three-phased course module, each of the phase designed to engage the participants and cater to their needs/interest while delving into specific data analysis and visualization tools.

Nepal Data Literacy Program 2019

World Bank, in collaboration with DFID Nepal, launched the Nepal Data Literacy program in mid-2019. First of its kind, the three-phased 100-hour program trained over 80 mid-career professionals from diverse backgrounds-- academia, civil society, private sector, media and international organizations-- to proactively engage in evidence-based policymaking and contribute in building stronger institutions. The program focused on both producers and consumers of data, making it possible to engage a cross-section of decision-makers, emphasize their role and utility of data-driven decision-making at national and sub-national level in Nepal.

The program equipped and empowered the participants with various data analysis and visualization tools along with leadership development and design thinking concepts.

Program Impact

By the end, graduates of the Nepal Data Literacy Program had harnessed an aptitude to first, make better data-driven decisions in their everyday life and then, ‘pay it forward’ by transferring the acquired knowledge and skills to others in their network through trainings and workshops. Within the first five months of the program, the participants had trained over 100 people within their communities or organizations.

All the resources and toolkits used during the program is published in an open source Data Literacy portal to help users develop their data skills.With over 28,000 global reach within six months of launch, the content developed and published on the Data Literacy portal have been used freely and seamlessly both within and outside of Nepal.

Following the completion of the program, Kathmandu University School of Management (KUSOM) is partnering with Nepal Data Literacy Program to incorporate data-literacy toolkits in their BBA and MBA program and develop a data-driven course, freely and openly available to other institutions and thousands of students. Similarly, British College in Nepal has incorporated components of Nepal Data Literacy Program in its existing courses. Altogether, over the next three years, over 1,400 undergraduate and graduate students are expected to be data literate as a result of Nepal Data Literacy Program.

Furthermore, to integrate data literacy with education, KUSOM is organizing a training for teachers from outside of Kathmandu valley and working with them to transfer knowledge and skills to their respective schools and communities.
